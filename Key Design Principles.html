Key Design Principles - Chase Evans

Design Principles List of Considerations
Key Design Principles
  • Visibility
  • Consistancy
  • Constraints
  • Feedback
  • Affordances
  • Accessibility
Complexity Principles
  • Some disabilites dont allow people to interact in certain ways
  • Complexity is necessary for systems with lots of functionality
  • To simplify the user's interaction requires more complexity behind the scenes
  • For any system there is a certain amount of complexity which cannot be reduced
  • Allow users to learn the system by doing (rather than requiring lengthy tutorials)
  • Help users adopt more efficient methods in-context learning, tool tips, etc
  • Provide fluid pathways
  • Reduce clutter without reducing functionality
Cultural/Gender Principles
  • Different cultures speak different languages
  • Some cultures read right to left instead of left to right
  • Choose colors carefully during design process
  • Certain symbols have different meanings across cultures
  • Dont rely on auto translation
  • Words may carry different meanings in other cultures
  • Individualism vs communitarianism
  • Designing for gender has considerations:
    • Different colors for gendered products
    • Different styles of advertizing
    • Different shapes for the product if its physical
    • Self care products have different scents
Conceptional Principles
  • Jakobs law: users spend most of their time on other sites, this means that users prefer your site to work the same way as all the other sites they know
  • Users transfer expectations they have built from other services to yours
  • Legacy Problem: too many devices use the existing standard
  • Utilize mental models
Emotional Principles
  • Keep users emotions in mind during design process
  • Adults and children have different sensitivities
  • You want your product to evoke a preplanned emotion from your user
  • Choose colors, fonts, and planned emotion before the design process starts
  • Emotions can be very nuansed and complex so careful consideration is required
  • Different culture portray emotions differently
  • Haptic feedback is a great way to connect the user emotionally to the product, example being a cell phones vibration
Human Centered Design Principles
  • Utilize scenarios to try and predict how users would interact with software
  • Put human needs, capabilities and behavior first
  • Design to meet those needs
  • Good communication between people and computers are required
  • Consider how and when things may go wrong
  • Provide Feedback
  • Use a conceptional model
  • Include affordances, signifiers and constraints
  • ensure you have good mapping
User Interface Design Principles
  • Dont make users think
  • Build webpages for scanning not reading
  • Use visual hierarches, break up pages into clearly designed areas
  • Make whats clickable obvious
  • Avoid clutter and disorganization
  • Avoid using disruptive noises
  • Instructions should be brief and timely
  • Omit needless words
Interface Design Principles
  • Design with consistency in mind
  • Keep the interface simple and have a sense of clarity
  • Give effective feedback and affordances to the users
  • The interface should enable efficiency and productivity
  • Mobile Device Design Principles:
    • Scalable design for various phone sizes
    • Allow zooming to ensure your site doesnt resist a mobile display
    • Design for easy use with either 1 or 2 hands
    • Allow for orientation in either left or right hand
    • Make important buttons or features large and noticeable
    • Design app to be held either vertically or horizontally
    • Address Hicks Law:
      • As number of options increases, the users reaction time slows
      • K.I.S.S. (Keep It Short and Simple)
Cognitive Design Principles Attention:
  • Minimize cognitive load
  • Use visual hierarchy
  • Feedback and notifications
  • User-centered default settings
Perception:
  • Consistency in design
  • Feedback through small animation
  • Chunk relevant information
  • Enable effective contrast and readability
Memory:
  • Clear labels and icons
  • Effective use of error handling
  • Utilize progressive disclosure
  • Affordances and signifiers
Error Principles
  • Make clear error messages
  • Enable users to recover from errors fast and smooth
  • Have error handling be consistent
  • When dealing with errors focus on the users perspective
